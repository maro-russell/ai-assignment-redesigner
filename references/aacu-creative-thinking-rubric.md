# AAC&U Creative Thinking VALUE Rubric

**Source:** Association of American Colleges and Universities (AAC&U)  
**Contact:** value@aacu.org

---

## Definition

Creative thinking is both the capacity to **combine or synthesize existing ideas, images, or expertise in original ways** and the experience of thinking, reacting, and working in an imaginative way characterized by a high degree of **innovation, divergent thinking, and risk taking**.

---

## Framing Notes for Assignment Design

Creative thinking in higher education must be distinguished from unfocused creativity. It can only be expressed productively within a **particular domain**. The student must have a strong foundation in domain strategies and skills in order to make connections and synthesize. At the highest performance levels, the creative thinker pushes beyond domain boundaries in new, unique, or atypical recombinations — uncovering new syntheses and using or recognizing creative risk-taking to achieve a solution.

This rubric is intended for **transdisciplinary or interdisciplinary work samples**. Applicable work types include: research papers, lab reports, musical compositions, mathematical solutions, prototype designs, reflective pieces, or other academic works completed by individuals or groups.

*Evaluators are encouraged to assign a zero to any work that does not meet Benchmark (level 1) performance.*

---

## Rubric: Six Dimensions × Four Levels

### 1. Acquiring Competencies
*Acquiring strategies and skills within a particular domain.*

| Level | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **Capstone (4)** | **Reflect:** Evaluates creative process and product using domain-appropriate criteria. |
| **Milestone 3** | **Create:** Creates an entirely new object, solution, or idea that is appropriate to the domain. |
| **Milestone 2** | **Adapt:** Successfully adapts an appropriate exemplar to his/her own specifications. |
| **Benchmark (1)** | **Model:** Successfully reproduces an appropriate exemplar. |

---

### 2. Taking Risks
*May include personal risk (fear of embarrassment or rejection) or risk of failure — e.g., going beyond original parameters, introducing new materials/forms, tackling controversial topics, advocating unpopular ideas.*

| Level | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **Capstone (4)** | Actively seeks out and follows through on untested and potentially risky directions or approaches in the final product. |
| **Milestone 3** | Incorporates new directions or approaches to the assignment in the final product. |
| **Milestone 2** | Considers new directions or approaches without going beyond the guidelines of the assignment. |
| **Benchmark (1)** | Stays strictly within the guidelines of the assignment. |

---

### 3. Solving Problems

| Level | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **Capstone (4)** | Not only develops a logical, consistent plan to solve the problem, but recognizes consequences of the solution and can articulate reason for choosing it. |
| **Milestone 3** | Having selected from among alternatives, develops a logical, consistent plan to solve the problem. |
| **Milestone 2** | Considers and rejects less acceptable approaches to solving the problem. |
| **Benchmark (1)** | Only a single approach is considered and used to solve the problem. |

---

### 4. Embracing Contradictions

| Level | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **Capstone (4)** | Integrates alternate, divergent, or contradictory perspectives or ideas **fully**. |
| **Milestone 3** | Incorporates alternate, divergent, or contradictory perspectives or ideas in an **exploratory** way. |
| **Milestone 2** | Includes (recognizes the value of) alternate, divergent, or contradictory perspectives or ideas in a **small** way. |
| **Benchmark (1)** | Acknowledges (mentions in passing) alternate, divergent, or contradictory perspectives or ideas. |

---

### 5. Innovative Thinking
*Novelty or uniqueness of idea, claim, question, form, etc.*

| Level | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **Capstone (4)** | Extends a novel or unique idea, question, format, or product to **create new knowledge or knowledge that crosses boundaries**. |
| **Milestone 3** | Creates a novel or unique idea, question, format, or product. |
| **Milestone 2** | Experiments with creating a novel or unique idea, question, format, or product. |
| **Benchmark (1)** | Reformulates a collection of available ideas. |

---

### 6. Connecting, Synthesizing, Transforming

| Level | Descriptor |
|-------|-----------|
| **Capstone (4)** | Transforms ideas or solutions into **entirely new forms**. |
| **Milestone 3** | Synthesizes ideas or solutions into a **coherent whole**. |
| **Milestone 2** | Connects ideas or solutions in **novel ways**. |
| **Benchmark (1)** | Recognizes **existing connections** among ideas or solutions. |

---

## Quick Reference: Student-Friendly Language for Each Dimension

Use these translations when writing assignment criteria for students:

| Rubric Dimension | Student-Friendly Equivalent |
|-----------------|---------------------------|
| Acquiring Competencies | "Show you understand the rules of the field — then break them intentionally" |
| Taking Risks | "Try something that might not work. Explain why you tried it." |
| Solving Problems | "Don't just pick the first solution. Compare options and defend your choice." |
| Embracing Contradictions | "Find the strongest argument against your idea — and engage with it seriously." |
| Innovative Thinking | "Bring something new. It doesn't have to be radical — but it has to be yours." |
| Connecting, Synthesizing, Transforming | "Don't just list ideas. Show how they change each other when brought together." |

## Glossary (AAC&U)
- **Exemplar:** A model or pattern to be copied or imitated.
- **Domain:** Field of study or activity; a sphere of knowledge and influence.
